About

Through the Intensive Studies Program, I set out to create something that would answer my original question: How can I use technology to help those in medicine?

To do this, I decided to make a image classification model that classfied whether lung scans were benign or malignant for pneumonia. To do this, I used a dataset from Guangzhou Women and Children's Medical Center in China. This dataset contained 5,863 images of pediatric lung scans. Using this dataset, I began to do binary classification. However, it is important to note that the malignant data included scans of both viral and bacterial pneumonia.

I began this project in Summer 2024, when I attend Carnegie Mellon University's AI Scholars Program. I learned the foundations of machine learning and ended up making a model named RETINAI. Following this, I began to research in Fall 2024 to figure out exactly what I wanted to do for my ISP. Then, from Winter 2024-Spring 2025, I began to create my model on VSCode.
